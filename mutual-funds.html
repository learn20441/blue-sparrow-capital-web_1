What is a Mutual Fund?

A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests it in a diversified portfolio of securities like equities, bonds, money-market instruments or a mix. A professional fund manager handles research, selection and rebalancing. You can invest lumpsum or via SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), and buy/sell at the fund’s NAV.

  • • Diversification reduces single-stock risk
  • • Professional management & compliance
  • • Liquidity (open-ended funds redeemable on any business day)
  • • Goal-based investing through SIP/STP/SWP

How it works

  1. You invest (SIP or Lumpsum).
  2. Fund house issues units at NAV.
  3. Manager invests per the scheme mandate.
  4. Returns accrue from market movement & income (dividends/interest).
  5. Redeem units at prevailing NAV (exit load may apply).

Why invest in Mutual Funds?

  • • Start small with SIP; rupee-cost averaging
  • • Diversification across many securities
  • • Transparent—daily NAVs & factsheets
  • • Professionally managed & regulated
  • • Works for multiple goals as per scheme

Know the risks & costs

  • • Market risk: NAVs fluctuate
  • • Interest-rate & credit risk in debt funds
  • • Expense Ratio (TER), Exit Loads may apply
  • • Tax treatment depends on category/holding period

FAQs

What’s the difference between SIP and Lumpsum?

SIP invests a fixed amount at regular intervals and averages the purchase cost. Lumpsum is a one-time investment—best with long horizon.

How do I choose a fund?

Match fund type to your goal, risk tolerance and time horizon. Check mandate, allocation, costs, and long-term track record.

Are mutual funds guaranteed?

No—mutual funds are market-linked. Capital protection isn’t guaranteed unless specified by the product.
