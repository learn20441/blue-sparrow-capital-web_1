Portfolio Management Services (PMS)

PMS is a premium, personalized investment service where a dedicated fund manager actively manages your portfolio in your own name. The strategy is tailored to your risk profile, goals, and tax situation, often with a focused set of high-conviction holdings.

What is PMS?

In PMS, every client has a separate Demat and bank account, and each transaction is executed in the client’s own name. PMS portfolios are typically concentrated (20–30 stocks) to reflect high-conviction ideas. Reporting is transaction-level, offering greater transparency and control.

Types of PMS

Discretionary PMS

The portfolio manager takes buy/sell decisions on your behalf as per the agreed strategy and mandate. Best for clients who prefer professional, end-to-end management.

Non-Discretionary PMS

The manager provides recommendations, but final execution happens only after your approval. Suits investors who want control over orders.

Advisory PMS

The manager only gives advice; execution remains with you. Useful when you want guidance without granting execution authority.

PMS Onboarding Process

1
Discovery & Suitability

We discuss goals, risk appetite, time horizon, and liquidity needs. A suitability assessment ensures PMS is appropriate for you.

2
Documentation

PMS agreement, KYC, and disclosures are completed. You receive scheme details, fee structure, and risk factors in writing.

3
Account Setup

Your dedicated Demat and linked bank account are set up. Execution authorization (PoA/TPIN) is configured securely.

4
Funding / Transfer

You fund the account (cash or approved securities). Minimum ticket is typically ₹50 lakh as per prevailing norms.

5
Portfolio Deployment

The manager deploys capital per strategy—phased entries, position sizing, and risk controls—with ongoing monitoring and reports.

PMS Strategy Types

Large-Cap Quality

Leaders with robust balance sheets and predictable cash flows; typically lower drawdowns.

  • Core compounding
  • Lower turnover

Mid/Small-Cap Growth

High-growth themes with higher potential alpha—and higher volatility.

  • Secular trends
  • Active risk control

Thematic / Blend

Manufacturing, financials, consumption, or a flexible blend aligned to your goals.

  • Dynamic allocation
  • Goal-based

Taxation (Equity-Oriented Example)

How gains are taxed

  • LTCG (held > 12 months): 10% on gains exceeding ₹1 lakh in a financial year
  • STCG (held ≤ 12 months): 15%
  • Dividends: Taxed as per investor’s slab

Gains are computed based on actual trade dates in your account.

Illustration

Suppose equity shares bought in April 2024 are sold in June 2025 at a profit of ₹2,50,000. After the ₹1,00,000 annual exemption, ₹1,50,000 is taxed at 10% (plus applicable surcharge & cess).

Risks & Considerations

Market Risk

Equity-oriented portfolios can be volatile; near-term drawdowns are possible.

Concentration Risk

Focused portfolios (20–30 stocks) may carry higher stock-specific risk.

Liquidity & Costs

Execution, brokerage, taxes, and exit timing can impact realized returns.

Important: Past performance is not indicative of future returns. No returns are guaranteed. Read scheme documents carefully.
